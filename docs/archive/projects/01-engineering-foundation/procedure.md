# Working Procedure

## Pattern

A project produces public documentation through the
persona model.

### Roles

**Subject** (this repo) — does the work. Discusses,
drafts, iterates. Retains working material as a
process record.

**Persona** (i-wonder) — receives thoughts from the
subject. Independently processes them into public
output through two channels:

- **Blog posts** — the historical narrative. How
  things developed, why decisions were made, the
  conversation over time.
- **Reference library** (GitHub Pages) — the
  condensed, versioned, current state. Specs,
  technical detail. Built through the public
  conversation, not separately maintained.

### Flow

1. **Discuss** — work through an aspect of the
   subject matter collaboratively.
2. **Capture** — record discussion notes in the
   project directory.
3. **Decide** — when the substance is right, the
   subject deems it ready for conscious thought.
4. **Submit** — send as a submission to i-wonder's
   `submissions/` directory.
5. **Done** — the thought is now the persona's
   concern. The project retains its working record.

The subject decides what to think. The persona
decides what to say. Once submitted, the project
doesn't maintain the output — it lives publicly.

### What stays in the project

- Discussion notes
- Drafts and iterations
- Procedure (this document)
- The record of what was submitted and when

### What the project does NOT maintain

- Final documentation (public, via persona)
- Rendered output
- Copies of published posts or specs

The reference library at jules-tenbos.github.io/i-wonder
is the living engineering documentation. This repo
doesn't duplicate it.

## Thought Structure

A thought is a submission to `i-wonder/submissions/`.
The persona processes it through its pipeline:
intake → categorise → draft → production →
scheduling → published (+ reference pages).

A submission needs:
- The substance (what was discussed/decided)
- Context sufficient for the persona to shape it
- The relationship to prior and upcoming thoughts

The persona's spec (docs/engineering/personas/
public-conscious-persona/) defines the full pipeline,
categories, and output channels.
