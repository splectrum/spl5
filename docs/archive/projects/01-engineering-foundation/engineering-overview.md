# Splectrum — Engineering Overview

## Seed Principles

Five principles ground all engineering. They originate
from the philosophical work and apply to every language
game (API, protocol, interaction) in the system.

- **P1** Language is relational
- **P2** Language is the medium through which a subject
  experiences reality
- **P3** Language is where subjects share knowledge
  about reality
- **P4** Languages are inter-relational with equal
  standing in potential
- **P5** Together they form a web of growing complexity

## Components

### Mycelium

The fabric of the information world. Data structures,
metadata structures, protocol infrastructure.

Mycelium provides the means to construct subject
repos — the material everything is made from. It
defines how data is organised, how protocols are
registered and resolved, how contexts relate to each
other through references.

### Subject

A subject repo is a specific infilling of mycelium
fabric — a git-backed repository structured for a
specific set of activities, filled with its own data
and protocols.

A subject is an entity's information world. It cannot
be separated from its context — the subject and its
world are one thing. From inside: a complete reality
with its own POV. From outside: a participant in the
wider information world whose actions affect other
subjects.

### HAICC

The collaboration model for activities where human
and AI work together. Governs the distribution of
work across human, principal AI, and AI agents.

HAICC lives within activities, not within subjects
as a whole. Each activity has its own HAICC profile:
the logical/physical boundary, the level of AI
autonomy, the interaction pattern. These profiles
evolve as capabilities grow.

### Persona

A communication channel — a subject whose activities
produce public output. A persona has its own voice,
its own editorial judgement, its own form of life.

A persona is made from mycelium fabric (it is a
subject repo). Its activities are designed with HAICC
(the collaboration model governs how work distributes
across participants).

### Splectrum

The common language. What all APIs, protocols, and
interactions share. The seed principles expressed as
engineering constraints.

Splectrum is not a component alongside the others —
it is what they have in common. Its engineering spec
will emerge from the evidence of building the others.

## How They Interact

```
Splectrum (seed principles)
    governs all
        │
        ├── Mycelium (fabric)
        │       constructs
        │           │
        │           ├── Subject (repos)
        │           │       ↕ interact through
        │           │     protocols (language games)
        │           │       │
        │           │       ├── actions change data
        │           │       └── data changes reshape
        │           │           other subjects' reality
        │           │
        │           └── Persona (communication subjects)
        │                   receives thoughts from subjects
        │                   produces public output
        │
        └── HAICC (collaboration model)
                shapes activities within subjects
                    │
                    ├── logical (human steers meaning)
                    └── physical (AI acts autonomously)
```

Splectrum principles govern everything. Mycelium
fabric constructs subjects. Subjects interact through
protocols — actions on the information world that
reshape each other's reality. Personas are subjects
that communicate publicly. HAICC shapes how human and
AI collaborate within any activity.

## The Relational Dynamic

Subjects don't communicate directly. They act on the
information world through protocols, and those actions
change the subject context of other participants.

This is the engineering expression of RQM: properties
(data) exist through interaction (protocols), not in
isolation. There is no god's-eye view — only subject
contexts constructed by mycelium's web structure.

## Conscious and Unconscious

A subject's internal work is unconscious — active but
not surfaced. When the subject deems something worth
sharing, it becomes a conscious thought — submitted
to a persona.

The persona exercises its own judgement: publish,
suppress, defer, reshape. Two levels of agency: the
subject decides what to think; the persona decides
what to say.

The unconscious is not hidden — it can be
interrogated through cascading references if access
exists.
